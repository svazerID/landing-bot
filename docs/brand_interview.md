# Brand Discovery Interview: Inside a Head

This document captures the foundational brand interview that informed the identity, tone, and structure of the **Inside a Head** digital studio.

## Core Identity & Vision

**Q: The Origin Story: Why the name "Inside a Head"?**
**A:** I always lived inside my head and always made plans, came up with stories and cool ideas. So when I was thinking about a brand, that's the first idea that came to mind. Let's explore *inside a head*.

**Q: The "Studio" Label: Why a studio rather than a portfolio?**
**A:** It's more of a conceptual framework. I don't like the idea of a portfolio, it feels static with what has already been done. A studio is something active, where things are happening and it's always in motion. Plus it sounds cooler.

**Q: Core Philosophy on design and functionality?**
**A:** Design and functionality should always co-exist, and design should take humans and how they would use it into consideration.

**Q: The "Mess" vs. The "System": Is this tension the core of your process?**
**A:** This is an unintentional one but it's such a cool concept. I started building apps and found the design system where every component of a website/app is well defined and I really loved this idea, so I wanted to make one for myself. The idea of a *Museum of my own mess* comes from a song and I love it. I have so many ideas and hobbies and it feels like a museum when I look back at all the things I've started and abandoned.

## Professional Narrative

**Q: The Persona: PM, Musician, Builder. How do they rank?**
**A:** I'm an idealist. I see good things in bad things and bad things in good things. I worked as a PM and I really like the job, because you can create and influence things. The musician comes after; I've always loved music. I'm not the best musician by any chance, but I like how music makes you feel. Regarding the builder, I always wanted to create stuff with my own hands. So maybe: Idealist Builder, Visionary PM, Musician?

**Q: Unique Selling Point?**
**A:** I think my selling point is that I always find a way, and there's always a way to succeed, even if it's hard and takes a while to get going.

**Q: Target Audience?**
**A:** My initial idea was to make this my portfolio for potential recruiters, but right now I want to make it my place on the internet to come up with ideas.

**Q: Key Message?**
**A:** There are a lot of ideas out there, but you should actually *start building*.

## Design & Aesthetic Vibe

**Q: Inspiration?**
**A:** Vercel and their Geist design system. Also Monks (monks.com) – I really like them.

**Q: Brand Voice: What would the physical space look like?**
**A:** A messy coffee shop with a huge library.

**Q: Emotional Response from visitors?**
**A:** Inspired and intrigued. I always valued if someone felt intrigued after talking with me because that makes me memorable.

**Q: Visual Non-Negotiables?**
**A:** I like metaphors, they are kind of essential.

## Interaction & Content

**Q: The Logbook’s Intent?**
**A:** It is a bit of everything, I don't really know right now how to structure it yet. I'm open to suggestions.

**Q: The "Museum of My Mess" Goal?**
**A:** To show the human side of everything. 

**Q: Desired "Call to Action"?**
**A:** Let's connect and talk more about projects and ideas. Let's ideate together.

**Q: Technical Pride?**
**A:** It doesn't really matter because it's vibe coded. The result is what counts.

## Future & Context

**Q: Evolution: Where do you see "Inside a Head" in 2 years?**
**A:** If I could dream or let's say like a north star, I would love it to become an agency or something in regards to design, marketing, management, or even helping out smaller creators and builders to achieve their ideas.

**Q: Open Source/Audience?**
**A:** It's for me in the first place, and then it's for people who want to understand my ideas.

**Q: Content Velocity?**
**A:** I want it to be a living organism with weekly updates if possible.

**Q: One Word to summarize?**
**A:** Ideas and Messy.
